About Me

I am a senior systems and network engineer with an architectural focus on secure, resilient infrastructure. I design and implement server platforms and the networks that connect them, with particular expertise in VLAN segmentation, VPN architecture, and network isolation for enterprise, government, and regulated environments.

I have led the design and implementation of environments aligned with NIST security frameworks, helping clients meet regulatory requirements while maintaining eligibility for cyber-insurance coverage. I have worked directly with state government systems and have maintained CJIS/CJIN certification for many years, giving me deep familiarity with compliance-driven design and operational realities.

Beyond architecture and operations, I bring an adversarial mindset to security. I have professional experience with software-based red teaming, which informs how I approach segmentation, access control, monitoring, and recovery planning.

I emphasize automation, standardization, and long-term maintainability. I have built extensive tooling in PowerShell, Python, and Bash to reduce operational overhead and make complex systems easier to understand, audit, and recover.

Outside of work, I’m a lifelong nerd with a love for comic books and tabletop RPGs such as D&D, Fate/Fudge, and GURPS, and I pursue locksport as a hobby. I’m also an avid photographer and a fan of the natural landscapes of New England, spending time outdoors whenever I can. That appreciation for structure, detail, and perspective—whether in systems or in nature—shapes how I approach both my technical work and creative pursuits.
